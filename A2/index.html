Discoveries & Insights

Summary

In this exploratory data analysis we explore the population demographics and zoning distribution of different Boston-based municipalities in an attempt to better understand the complexities of Boston's zoning and housing supply.

We began by exploring the dataset, noting it's focus on population demographics and municipality zoning info. We then took a closer look at MBTA communities, and noted an unexpected exception due to zoning lows. After, we looked at the overall dominance of single family home zoning, and finally at general population data for residents in the Boston area.

Ultimately, we were able to create a clear picture of Boston's public transportation access, single-family residential zoning, and population demographics. In investigating our analysis questions, we discovered that an overwhelming majority of homes in the greater Boston area, single-family or not, are near the MBTA, making that an unlikely deciding factor with regards to residential zoning types. We were also able to see clearly that there are a fair number of existing single-family zones, despite inner Boston's high population density. Lastly, we were able to see that there exist both a large number of very wealthy residents, and residents between 18 and 64, which was a somewhat unexpected combination.
