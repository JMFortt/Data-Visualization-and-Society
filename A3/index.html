Design Critique:

I think the visualization does a good job of showing a repeating cyclic pattern of cases year-to-year (peaks in Jan., troughs in June). The spiral shape lends itself well to that, I think. However, despite the form lending itself to that purpose, I think it could be made clearer with chronological captions to describe events on the timeline (since at this point, many of the major milestones in COVID have a good chance of being common knowledge). As it stands, there is only one caption included, and that particular one could be made clearer with additional details/context, since I as the reader am left to infer what it means.

Additionally, I think the visualization could be a little clearer in its overall design - my first thought when I saw this was "where do I look first?" My eye was initially drawn to the largest peak, which happens to be at the top center of the page, just next to the legend. This, however, is the end of the spiral timeline, not the beginning. Tangentially, the spiral ballooning and shrinking, although it makes sense logically, is surprisingly difficult to read (despite the scale given). I'm curious if spiralling bars would be clearer?

Final Visualization Design

Final Writeup

In my final visualization, I decided to focus on visualizing the year-to-year trends of new confirmed COVID cases. Specifically, I wanted to answer the question "when do COVID cases historically peak and fall?" To do so, I decided to divide the numbers among the 12 months, and use a stacked bar chart to emphasize aggregate trends across years. I made the chart horizontal for easier top-down scan of peaks and troughs, and used color to differentiate cases from year to year. While I didn't have a firm choice of color encodings in mind originally, I wanted to emphasize increasing "preparedness" as the years progressed (regardless of how the numbers played out), so I made 2020 (the first year of COVID) red, and the subsequent year yellow.

I definitely think that my chosen visualization is easier to read and detect trends in than the original. While sadly not as artistic, I think it makes clear the yearly cyclic rise and fall of COVID cases, even without the cyclic visual format. It's certainly not perfect though, as a reader would have to work a bit to separate out each portion of the stacked bar chart, but for the message I was trying to communicate, I think it works well enough. The process of "critique by redesign" was certainly effective in clarifying the challenges of communicating data, and has left me curious what more I could do communication-wise with an interactive medium...
